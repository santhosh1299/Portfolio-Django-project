The charging cycle was meticulously executed in a controlled laboratory setting, employing a well-established protocol. The process commenced with a constant current (CC) mode at 1.5A until the battery voltage reached a precise 4.2V. Subsequently, the charging transitioned seamlessly into a constant voltage (CV) mode, persisting until the charge current dwindled to a minimal 20mA. This intricate procedure ensures the lithium-ion batteries are methodically charged, aligning with the stringent requirements of the experiment. For the discharge phase, a constant current (CC) level of 2A was applied with precision. The discharge continued until the battery voltage gracefully descended to predefined levels: 2.7V, 2.5V, 2.2V, and 2.5V for batteries 5, 6, 7, and 18, respectively. Each discharge cycle meticulously simulated real-world usage scenarios, capturing the dynamic behavior of the batteries under varying conditions. The iterative charge and discharge cycles intentionally induced accelerated aging of the batteries, offering a unique vantage point into their long-term performance characteristics. Concurrent impedance measurements provided invaluable insights into the evolving internal parameters of the batteries throughout the aging process. This dual approach allowed for a comprehensive understanding of how the batteries' performance and characteristics transformed over repeated cycles. The experiments adhered to rigorous standards and were meticulously monitored until the batteries reached their predefined end-of-life (EOL) criteria. This comprehensive methodology ensures that the obtained data accurately reflects the intricate interplay of factors influencing the aging process of lithium-ion batteries.
